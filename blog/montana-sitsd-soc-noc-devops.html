Montana SITSD: SOC/NOC/DevOps

June 2026 / Cyril Blyseth

At the State of Montana Department of Administration / SITSD, I worked in network operations and cybersecurity, then supervised a combined SOC/NOC/DevOps team. The environment supported 30k+ users and devices.

Scope

  • Led 11-14 SOC, NOC, and DevOps staff.
  • Supported enterprise operations across critical state services, agencies, networks, users, and devices.
  • Worked across security monitoring, incident response, outage response, network operations, DevOps, architecture review, and process improvement.

Automation and operations

  • Automated phishing workflows, outage reporting, IOC ingestion, VPN setup, alert routing, and other SOAR-style processes.
  • Built or improved reporting for critical services and network devices, including public-safety and high-availability environments.
  • Worked with Splunk, Palo Alto logs, SentinelOne, Nessus, Tenable.io, dashboards, patching, and network monitoring.
  • Built alerts, investigated phishing and network-detection events, blocked suspicious activity, and supported remediation work.
  • Helped manage vulnerability scanning and automated scanning processes.

Process work

  • Helped found the JCSOC effort across Montana, North Dakota, and South Dakota.
  • Built the State of Montana incident response manual for critical services and emergency processes using NIST 800-53 as a major reference point.
  • Rebuilt change management intake so requests were better templated, filtered, routed, and approved.
  • Rebuilt firewall access request intake and routing.
  • Helped build CMDB practices for the enterprise.
  • Ran technical architecture review.
  • Worked on hard-drive destruction/security processes, phishing simulations, staff training, and enterprise operations process work.

What I took from it

This is where I learned how much of technical work is actually the glue: the handoffs, approvals, intake quality, team ownership, missing context, and "who is allowed to do what" questions. A lot of my Agentic IT work comes from wanting those problems to have better tooling.
