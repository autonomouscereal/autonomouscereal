Titan / Zen: AI, cybersecurity, CI/CD automation

June 2026 / Cyril Blyseth

I am currently contracting through Titan Technologies / Zen Strategics on AI, cybersecurity, and CI/CD automation. I keep client-specific details off the public site, but the public version is simple: a lot of the work is about making security review, repository intake, scanner results, and AI/documentation workflows less manual and easier to act on.

What I work on

  • Automated CI/CD security review processes.
  • SAST scanning, manual SAST evaluation, and scanner output review.
  • Automated SAST scanning platforms and repeatable security-review workflows.
  • Repository ingestion across organizations: find repos, scan them, normalize results, and prioritize the work.
  • CVE and KEV correlation so findings are not just a giant flat list.
  • Report generation and prioritization for teams that need to know what actually matters first.
  • Technical advisory review for security, AI, ML, software risk, and approval criteria.
  • AI systems for training and documentation workflows.
  • Automation around documentation translation and SharePoint-style knowledge flows.

The shape of the work

The pattern is usually not "run a scanner and paste the output." The useful part is the work around the scanner: 

repos
  -> inventory
  -> scan
  -> normalize findings
  -> correlate CVEs / KEVs / context
  -> prioritize
  -> write the report
  -> route through human review

That is also why this experience connects directly to my Agentic IT and CI/CD security work. The hard part is not just finding issues. The hard part is making the next action clear without pretending automation should be the final authority.

What it proves

  • I can work in environments where AI, security, compliance, and real review gates overlap.
  • I care about evidence, repeatability, and making reports useful to the people who have to act on them.
  • I can build automation around messy organizational inputs without exposing private client details.
